LIGHT CITY SMP — Official 2v2 Tournament Rulebook

Prize Pool: $70.00 | Format: 2v2

By registering, you acknowledge and agree to every rule listed in this document.

1. General Conduct & SMP Rules

  • All standard Light City SMP server rules are in full effect throughout the entire tournament, including warm-ups, practices, and post-match periods.
  • Disruptive behavior, trolling, intentional griefing, or refusing to follow admin orders will result in immediate removal from the tournament with no refund or compensation.
  • Any form of harassment or targeted toxicity toward other players, spectators, or staff is forbidden. General competitive banter is fine — targeted abuse is not.
  • Teammates are equally responsible for each other's conduct. If one player is removed for rule violations, their partner is also eliminated.
  • By signing up, you formally agree to all rules listed here. Ignorance of the rules is not a valid excuse. If you don't agree, don't register.

2. Client Modifications & PC Checks

  • Cheating, exploiting, or use of any unauthorized client modifications is strictly prohibited. This includes kill aura, reach hacks, auto-clicker, anti-knockback, or anything that gives an unfair advantage.
  • Approved mods include: Optifine, FPS-boosting mods, brightness/gamma mods, and minimaps that do not show players or entities. If you're unsure whether your mod is allowed, ask an admin before the tournament — not during.
  • You must explicitly consent to a full PC check by Light City SMP administration before joining. Refusing = automatic disqualification.
  • If suspicious gameplay is observed, an additional PC check may be requested at any point. Refusal at that stage also results in immediate disqualification.
  • Any player found cheating will be permanently banned from all future Light City SMP tournaments.

3. Team Composition & Registration

  • Teams must be exactly 2 players. Solo entries will not be accepted.
  • Rosters are locked once the bracket is published. No substitutions after that point, for any reason.
  • Each player may only be on one team. Playing for multiple teams or standing in for another team will get both teams disqualified.
  • Register using your exact in-game Minecraft usernames as they appear on the server.
  • All players must already be members of the Light City SMP server before the registration deadline.

4. Match Integrity & Combat

  • Going AFK, logging off, disconnecting intentionally, or stalling mid-fight is forbidden. The match will not be paused for any of these.
  • If you drop a weapon or item during combat, that's on you. No redos, no pauses, no item recovery.
  • Pearling to arena borders to heal, glitching outside the designated area, or exploiting any map bugs is prohibited.
  • Any PvP outside the designated arena results in a formal warning. Second offense = permanent removal from the tournament.
  • Throwing matches, teaming with your opponents, or colluding to manipulate bracket results will get all parties involved disqualified immediately.
  • You may not use consumables, potions, or gear that wasn't approved in the tournament loadout. No outside items in the arena.

5. Connectivity & Proof Recording

  • All players must record their gameplay at all times during the tournament. If you can't record, your application will be denied.
  • If you disconnect or lag out during a match, you must provide video proof immediately. No proof = the result stands, no exceptions.
  • Your recording software must be running before you enter the arena. "I forgot to record" is not grounds for a redo.
  • Recordings must show your full screen. Cropped or partial recordings will not be accepted as valid proof in any dispute.
  • If both players on a team disconnect due to a verified server-side issue, an admin may choose to replay the match at their discretion. This is not guaranteed.

6. Communication

  • All participants must be in the designated Discord tournament voice channel during their matches. Non-negotiable.
  • Failure to join within 5 minutes of your match being called = forfeit warning. 10 minutes = full forfeit.
  • You can talk to your teammate in a private call, but you must remain audible in the tournament voice channel for admin communication.
  • Spectators are not allowed to feed callouts or info to players mid-match. First offense is a warning, second is disqualification.
